# Enterprise Trust

For larger B2B, compliance-heavy, procurement-sensitive, multi-stakeholder, or credibility-first websites.

## Core Goal

Project control, maturity, and credibility without looking lifeless.

## Audience Pattern

- procurement teams
- executives
- compliance or IT stakeholders
- larger organizations with longer buying cycles

## Site Behavior

- clarity and confidence over flash
- proof must look durable and serious
- trust signals should feel institutional, not startup-hyped
- pages should feel composed, orderly, and credible

## Page Role Expectations

- Home: positioning + trust + capability overview
- Solutions/Services: capability mapping + proof
- About: institutional credibility and leadership
- Blog/Insights: authority building
- Contact/Sales: clear enterprise contact path

## Composition Tendencies

- moderate drama, never loud chaos
- cleaner section transitions
- more structured information groupings
- more serious proof treatment than launch-style pages
- CTA pressure lower than startup acquisition pages

## Proof Style

Prioritize:

- certifications
- security/compliance signals
- case-study outcomes
- client logos
- process maturity
- leadership and delivery credibility

## CTA Strategy

- primary CTA should feel serious and business-appropriate
- secondary CTA often points to contact, resources, or case studies
- repeated CTA should be more restrained than B2B SaaS marketing

## Recommended Mode Pairings

- `Clean SaaS`
- `Bold Marketing` with restraint
- `Dense Data` for capability or comparison-heavy pages

## Recommended Preset Pairings

- `Neutral Professional`
- `Technical Sharp`
- `Editorial Premium` when authority should feel more premium than technical

## What To Avoid

- startup-style visual hype everywhere
- playful warmth when the audience expects seriousness
- overuse of gradients, badges, and floating cards
- weak proof hierarchy

## Review Questions

- Does the site feel credible to a risk-sensitive buyer?
- Are proof and process maturity visible enough?
- Does the design avoid both blandness and hype?
