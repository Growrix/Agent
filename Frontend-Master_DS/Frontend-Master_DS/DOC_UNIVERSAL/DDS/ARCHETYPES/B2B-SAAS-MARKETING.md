# B2B SaaS Marketing

For startup SaaS, workflow tools, AI products, platform products, and conversion-oriented software websites.

## Core Goal

Explain the product clearly, prove credibility, and move the visitor toward signup, demo, or trial.

## Audience Pattern

- founders
- operators
- internal teams
- technical evaluators
- budget owners

## Site Behavior

- product understanding comes before decoration
- proof and product value must appear early
- every page should reduce evaluation friction
- messaging must feel sharper than a generic startup landing page

## Page Role Expectations

- Home: category framing + product proof + conversion
- Features/Services: explain product workflows and outcomes
- About: credibility and team quality
- Blog: education and acquisition support
- Contact/Sales: qualification and conversion

## Composition Tendencies

- hero with product angle and concrete value
- visible product proof in early viewport
- alternating narrative and proof sections
- comparison, ROI, or workflow explanation when relevant
- stronger CTA hierarchy than local service sites

## Proof Style

Prioritize:

- product visuals
- workflow explanation
- customer logos
- case-study metrics
- feature comparisons
- integrations and compatibility

## CTA Strategy

- one clear primary conversion path
- optional demo or contact path for high-consideration buyers
- repeated CTA okay if value proof escalates with it

## Recommended Mode Pairings

- `Bold Marketing` for homepage and feature pages
- `Dense Data` for pricing and comparison
- `Editorial Narrative` for case studies or thought leadership

## Recommended Preset Pairings

- `Technical Sharp`
- `Neutral Professional`
- `High-Energy Launch` for aggressive launch pages only

## What To Avoid

- vague startup slogans with no product clarity
- endless feature-card walls with no product evidence
- using launch-page energy across every page
- treating About as an internal team page with no business value

## Review Questions

- Is the product understandable from the first screen?
- Does the page show how the product works, not just what it claims?
- Are proof and CTA progression stronger than a generic SaaS template?
