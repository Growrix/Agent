# Local Service Lead Gen

For installer, contractor, trade, clinic, local professional, and regional service business websites.

## Core Goal

Turn trust into enquiries without feeling like a cheap local-business template.

## Audience Pattern

- homeowners
- local businesses
- regional buyers
- people comparing a small shortlist of providers

## Site Behavior

- trust appears early
- proof appears before deep storytelling
- contact paths are always easy to find
- service clarity matters more than abstract brand storytelling
- CTA density is moderate to high, but not spammy

## Page Role Expectations

- Home: trust + service overview + conversion
- Services: explanation + qualification + conversion
- About: credibility + team + values + reassurance
- Blog: education + SEO + trust support
- Contact: lowest-friction conversion page

## Composition Tendencies

- strong but practical hero
- visible proof band early
- clear service segmentation
- short explanation blocks over long narrative sections
- at least one strong conversion close on every core page
- light or softly tinted surfaces for the default public experience
- spacious section rhythm over dense app-like stacking

## Proof Style

Prioritize:

- certifications
- years in business
- completed jobs
- ratings
- local service area coverage
- testimonials with places and job context

## CTA Strategy

- one primary CTA across the site
- one secondary CTA for lower-commitment exploration
- contact CTA repeated intentionally, not in every small block

## Recommended Mode Pairings

- `Bold Marketing` for the overall site
- `Editorial Narrative` only as a light secondary influence for About or Blog

## Recommended Preset Pairings

- `Warm Human` for homeowner-friendly trust
- `Technical Sharp` for engineering-led authority
- `Neutral Professional` when a safer corporate tone is required

## What To Avoid

- generic 3-card hero + icon-grid + testimonials + CTA formula with no pacing
- overly app-like mobile UI patterns on a service website
- dark, dense, dashboard-like public pages unless the brief explicitly asks for them
- too many identical centered heading stacks
- too much abstract startup language and too little service clarity
- making Contact feel like a brand page instead of a conversion page

## Review Questions

- Can a user understand what the company installs or services within 5 seconds?
- Is local trust visible before the page gets too long?
- Do Services and Contact feel more useful than decorative?
- Does the site feel professional without becoming cold or generic?
