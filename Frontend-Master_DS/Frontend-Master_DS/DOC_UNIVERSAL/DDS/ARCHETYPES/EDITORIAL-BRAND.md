# Editorial Brand

For content-led brands, magazine-like public sites, knowledge-first products, thought leadership platforms, and narrative-heavy company sites.

## Core Goal

Build distinct voice, reading rhythm, and narrative trust without sacrificing usability.

## Audience Pattern

- readers
- subscribers
- content-driven buyers
- thoughtful evaluators
- people who need persuasion through depth, not just CTA pressure

## Site Behavior

- typography matters more than interface chrome
- rhythm and pacing matter more than card density
- proof may arrive later if the narrative earns it
- pages can breathe more than conversion-led archetypes

## Page Role Expectations

- Home: editorial front page or story-led brand entry
- Services/Offerings: narrative explanation with selective proof
- About: voice, mission, and authorial credibility
- Blog: a first-class destination, not a side section
- Contact: simpler and quieter than a lead-gen archetype

## Composition Tendencies

- stronger hierarchy through typography
- more varied reading rhythm
- selective use of imagery and pullout moments
- fewer identical card sections
- more section contrast through layout pacing than visual noise

## Proof Style

Prioritize:

- featured writing
- thought leadership
- strong author identity
- selective metrics and outcomes
- quotes and editorial proof moments

## CTA Strategy

- restrained, context-aware CTA use
- subscription, contact, or explore actions often beat hard-sell CTA repetition

## Recommended Mode Pairings

- `Editorial Narrative`
- `Bold Marketing` only for homepage hero or campaign moments

## Recommended Preset Pairings

- `Editorial Premium`
- `Warm Human`
- `Neutral Professional` when the brand must stay more reserved

## What To Avoid

- turning content pages into app dashboards
- identical boxed sections with no reading rhythm
- hard-selling every page equally
- over-decoration that weakens the editorial voice

## Review Questions

- Does the site feel authored rather than assembled?
- Does the content rhythm stay varied and readable?
- Is the CTA strategy appropriately restrained for an editorial experience?
